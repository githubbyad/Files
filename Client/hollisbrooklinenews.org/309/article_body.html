From the principal's desk...

I hope you’ve all had a great week despite the stretch of cold weather we’ve been experiencing. I've found that this time of year brings a special energy to our school—holiday spirit in the hallways, students supporting one another, and a sense of excitement as we head into the final stretch of 2025.
 
Tonight at 7:00 PM, we are thrilled to welcome our community to Guitar Night, featuring an incredible lineup of ’90s and early 2000s music performed entirely by our students—with a few surprise faculty guests who may take the stage as well! This event continues to be a highlight of the year, showcasing not only the talents of our musicians but also the impressive work happening behind the scenes. With Chazz Rogers directing the music and the entire technical production being run by students, supported by our dedicated Tech Club Advisor, Ben Michon, it is sure to be an unforgettable night! Tickets will be available at the door—we hope to see you there for a fun and nostalgic evening.
 
This week also offered a meaningful opportunity to connect with our entire school community. I had the chance to speak with students about our Advisory theme of the month: the idea of “Get to.” This mindset invites us to shift from viewing tasks as obligations to recognizing them as opportunities—we get to learn, grow, show up for one another, and take part in the many experiences our school offers. I shared this message with our 9th and 10th graders on Tuesday and with our upperclassmen on Thursday. In both sessions, our students were attentive, respectful, and engaged. It felt truly energizing to bring each group together and reflect on gratitude as we approach the winter season.
 
With the holiday break just around the corner, I want to wish everyone a restorative and joyful time with family and friends. When we return, Quarter 2 and Semester 1 will quickly come to an end, and we encourage all students to stay focused, seek support when needed, and continue building positive habits as we head into the new year.
 
Have a wonderful weekend, and I hope to see many of you at Guitar Night tonight...or the basketball games!
 
Mr. Hess


NEASC Information

As part of our ongoing commitment to continuous improvement, HBHS is currently engaged in the New England Association of Schools and Colleges (NEASC) accreditation process. NEASC is an organization that supports schools in reflecting on their practices, celebrating their strengths, and identifying areas for growth to ensure that all students receive the best possible educational experience.
 
This year, we are in the self-reflection phase, preparing for our Collaborative Conference in March 2026. A key component of this phase is gathering feedback from our school community—including students, families, and staff.
 
We are asking all families to take a few minutes to complete the NEASC Family Survey. Your input is invaluable in helping us understand what’s working well and where we can continue to grow as a school. The insights you share will directly inform our reflection and planning as we work to make HBHS even stronger.
